New York City

Painting by Piet Mondrian

For 75 years, a Piet Mondrian painting titled New York City I has been hanging upside down in museums, including in Düsseldorf since 1980. Recently discovered by art historian Susanne Meyer-Büser, the painting's thickening grid suggests it should be flipped to resemble a dark sky, contrary to its current depiction of a simplified skyline. Notably, the artwork lacks Mondrian's signature, possibly indicating its unfinished state.
